About me: Hi! I am Xiang Zhang. My research interest is plant biology. My research topic is “Effects of nutrient conditions on haustorium formation on parasitic plants.” I love exploring the molecular mechanisms in plant-environment interactions.

EDUCATION AND RESEARCH

Ph.D. | Molecular Biology

Nara Institute of Science and Technology, Japan | 2018-2023

I received Ph.D. degree from Nara Institute of Science and Technology, Japan. I conducted my research at Yoshida-lab of Division of Biological Science and my research area is plant symbiosis, i.e., exploring the molecular mechanisms of nutritional effect on parasitism. I like doing molecular experiments with improved protocol and confocal observation in various biological processes.

Master | Ecology

Northeast Normal University, China | 2014-2017

I did field and greenhouse ecological experiment when I was a Master student. (1) Design, conduct filed experiments to investigate the defense traits and mechanisms of plant to herbivory. (2) Investigating the characteristics of plant diversity and its relationship with soil resources heterogeneity in grassland of northeast area of China

Bachelor | Agriculture

Northwest A&F University, China | 2010-2014

I started my research work as junior student. I did physiology experiments to study the protective effects of Ca2+ on tall Fescue under low-light stress.
